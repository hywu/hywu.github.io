Galaxy clusters

Why is the Universe accelerating? Why is gravity repulsive on cosmic scales? Is it because of dark energy, or does General Relativity break down?

Galaxy clusters are the highest density peaks in the Universe. I use weak gravitational lensing effect to measure their mass. The number density vs. mass of galaxy clusters reveals how cosmic structures evolve under the tug-of-war between dark matter and dark energy.

I use the data from the Dark Energy Survey, which has imaged 1/8 of the entire sky and thousands of galaxy clusters. I also develop theory and simulations to help us better understand the astrophysics of galaxy clusters.

Galaxy evolution

Why are galaxies not forming as many stars as they used to at the earlier Universe? What was going on before the peak of cosmic star formation history?

Star-forming galaxies are bright in ultra-violet, but if they are dusty they appear bright in far-infrared. Observing galaxies in all wavelengths would reveal star formation activities across cosmic time.

Planck, Herschel, and ALMA have revealed the far-infrared sky. CMB S4 will observe dusty galaxies in high definition, while JWST and SPHEREx will observe young stars. I develop simulations to interpret observation data and to understand how we optimally combine these experiments.

Hubble constant tension

Hubble constant describes the expansion rate of the Universe. It is one of the most important parameters in cosmology.

We are currently very puzzled: Early-universe experiments favor a lower expansion rate (67), while local experiments favor a higher one (73). Is it possible that we live in a low-density, fast-expanding Hubble bubble?

I use simulations to quantify the environment of our Galaxy, and I found that if the Hubble bubble is to resolve the problem, it has to be an unphysically low-density region. We are still unsure if we live in a low-density region, but Even if we do, it cannot resolve the Hubble tension.
