Research   

Hao-Yi (Heidi) Wu

 
 
 
 

At Caltech, I have been working with Dr. Olivier Doré on cosmic far-infrared background (CIB), which originates from high-redshift dusty star-forming galaxies and provides constraints on star formation history.  I have developed a physical mode for interpreting the CIB measured by Planck (paper).

At the University of Michigan, I worked with Professor Dragan Huterer on systematics involved in dark energy experiments.  I looked at the impact of theoretical uncertainties involved in the predictions of galaxy clustering and their impact on cosmology (paper).  I also worked with Professor Gus Evrard on the dynamics of cluster galaxies using simulations (paper).

As a graduate student at Stanford, I explored various systematic effects that will impact future galaxy cluster surveys, e.g. the Dark Energy Survey and the South Pole Telescope.  I also worked on dark matter N-body simulations and used them to understand the halo properties of galaxy clusters.  My Ph.D. advisor was Professor Risa Wechsler. 

The following are more detailed descriptions of my earlier projects in reverse chronological order.


RHAPSODY-G: Hydrodynamic Simulations of Galaxy Clusters

In a series of papers, we have explored the observable-mass scaling relation, covariance between observables, and galaxy populations (paper).

Collaborators: O. Hahn, D. Martizzi, A. Evrard, R. Teyssier, R. Wechsler


RHAPSODY: Re-simulated Halo Sample for Statistical Observable-Mass Distribution Study

The mass of galaxy clusters is an essential quantity in the context of both precision cosmology and galaxy/cluster formation.   In optical cluster surveys, cluster mass is often inferred from the galaxy content inside the cluster, which usually has a large scatter.  To understand the physical origin of this scatter and further calibrate the observable-mass distribution, I have performed  zoom-in N-body simulations of massive clusters to resolve the substructures. I would like to answer the question: Why do clusters of the same mass have such a diversity of galaxy content? (Paper I, Paper II)

Collaborators: R. Wechsler, O. Hahn, P. Behroozi, Y. Mao, A. Evrard


Dark Energy Constraints from Galaxy Clusters

I am interested in studying dark energy using galaxy cluster surveys. I applied the Fisher matrix technique to calculate the cosmological parameter constraints and analyze various systematic effects for imminent large surveys, e.g., the Dark Energy Survey.  On the observational side, I proposed how we can optimize the follow-up strategy to maximize the dark energy constraints (paper).  On the theoretical side, I calculated the requirement of the theoretical calibration of the dark matter halo statistics (paper).

Collaborators: R.Wechsler, E. Rozo, A. Zentner


Assembly Bias in Galaxy Cluster Surveys

Galaxy clusters are biased tracers of the underlying dark matter distribution.  The so called “halo bias” can provide useful information for calibrating the cluster mass.  However, I demonstrated that the dependence of halo bias on halo assembly history needs to be carefully calibrated to avoid systematic errors in cosmological parameter inferences. This paper also includes my favorite derivation of the Fisher error.

Collaborators: R.Wechsler, E. Rozo
